Medium content

Meduim content will either take up 100%, 50% or 66% of the screen depending on how wide the screen is. It is useful for bundling some information that is too large to fit into a small content box, but too small for a large box. They should be put next to a small content box.

A good way to think of them is to think of them as "Two Thirds" boxes.

Small content

Small content splits off into it's own columns that take up 100%, 50% or 33% of the screen depending on how wide it is. They are the most dynamic block and should be put together in sets of three.

A good way to think of them is to think of them as "Third" boxes.

Dominant Small content

Small content splits off into it's own columns that take up 100%, 100% or 33% of the screen depending on how wide it is. They are the most dynamic block and should be put together in sets of three.

This box acts like a third except for at medium sized screens, where it will still take up 100%. If you're doing a group of three small boxes, you should make either the first or third one a dominant box so you don't have huge gaps whenyou expand to a medium sized screen.

Full size content

Full size content always takes up 100% width of the page, so your can be assured that it will be big enough to fill your needs.

A good way to think of them is to think of them as "Full boxes" boxes. They will always be the biggest they can be.

Large content

Large content splits off into it's own columns that take up 100%, 100% or 50% of the screen depending on how wide it is. They should be put together in sets of two.

It's good to think of them as "Half" blocks.
